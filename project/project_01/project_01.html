History of Taj Mahal

One of seven wonders in the world.

The Taj Mahal is a magnificent white marbel mausoleum located in Agra, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Sha Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1653.

Legend has it that Sha Jahan was grief-stricken after Mumtaz Mahal's death during childbirth, and he wanted to create a memorial that reflected his lover for her. The Taj Mahal is considered one of the most beautiful buildings in the world and is a symbol of eternal love.

The architectural masterpiece was designed by the Persian architect Ustad Ahmad Lahori, blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. The main building is set in a large gerden with a reflecting pool, creating a stunning reflection of the Taj Mahal.

The construction involved thousands of skilled artisans, laborers, and craftsmen, and a variety of precious materials were sourced from different parts of the world. The white marbel was brought from Makrana in Rajasthan, and the semi-precious stones for intricate inlay work came from various regions.

The Taj Mahal has stood the test of time and has become a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Millions of visitors from around the globe visit this iconic monument each year to witness its beauty and learn about the enduring love story that inspired its creation.
